About This Project

What is this?

Every year since 1960, Singapore's Finance Minister delivers a Budget Speech outlining the government's fiscal priorities. This project analyses 65 years of these speeches to reveal how Singapore's policy focus evolved from post-independence survival to sustainability leadership.

The Data

  • 66 speeches from 1960 to 2025
  • 39,704 sentences analysed
  • 10 Finance Ministers: Goh Keng Swee, Lim Kim San, Hon Sui Sen, Goh Chok Tong, Richard Hu, Tony Tan, Lee Hsien Loong, Tharman Shanmugaratnam, Heng Swee Keat, Lawrence Wong
  • Data Source: Singapore Parliament Hansard

Methodology

Speeches were extracted from PDF Hansard reports, parsed into sentences, and classified by topic using pattern matching. Linguistic metrics (readability, sentence length) were calculated using standard NLP methods. All code is open-source.

Global References

Country and region mentions are identified using pattern matching with a curated list of 100+ countries and their common aliases. For each country, we search for:

  • Official names: "United States", "United Kingdom"
  • Common abbreviations: "US", "USA", "UK", "UAE"
  • Demonyms: "American", "British", "Chinese"
  • Major cities: "Tokyo", "London", "Beijing" (as proxies for their countries)
  • Historical names: "Malaya", "Burma", "Ceylon", "Soviet Union"

Searches use word boundary matching to avoid false positives (e.g., "china" as porcelain vs "China" the country). Results are aggregated by year and region to show Singapore's evolving global outlook across 65 years of budget speeches.

Key Findings

  • Policy Evolution: Defence dominated in the 1960s-70s (survival), Social Services surged in the 2000s-10s (maturity), Environment/Sustainability emerged in the 2020s
  • Crisis Response: Economic topics spiked during 1985 recession, 1997 Asian Financial Crisis, 2008 Global Financial Crisis, and 2020 COVID-19
  • Linguistic Trends: Speeches became more readable over time (Flesch score increased from ~40 in 1960s to ~60 in 2020s), with shorter sentences and clearer language
